We started in the trenches of Epic go-lives. We built a company from there.

15 years ago, our founders were the analysts and PMs pulling 14-hour days during health system implementations. They saw what worked, what didn't, and what the staffing industry kept getting wrong.

The short version

We were the people health systems called when their staffing vendor sent analysts who'd never touched Epic. After years of fixing other firms' mistakes, we decided to build something better.

The idea was simple: only place people who've actually done the work. Not "proficient" — certified. Not "experienced with EHR" — they've been through go-lives, they know what command center looks like at 3am, and they don't need hand-holding.

That same rigor now extends across IT staffing, clinical placement, and custom software development. The bar doesn't change depending on the role.

What we actually care about

Every staffing company says "quality" and "partnership." Here's what those words mean when we say them.

We'd rather lose a deal than place the wrong person.

If we don't have someone who fits, we'll tell you. We've turned down engagements because we didn't have the right match — and that's why clients come back. Every candidate gets technically screened by someone who's done the role, not just an HR check.

Your team, extended

We don't disappear after placement. Regular check-ins, performance tracking, and if someone isn't working out, we replace them. No argument.

Outcomes, not hours

We measure every engagement by what actually shipped — on-time delivery, adoption rates, system stability. Not billable hours.

One vendor for everything.

Most of our clients started with us for Epic staffing. Then they realized we could also handle their IT augmentation. Then clinical. Then software development.

Now they've got one contract, one account manager, and one vetting standard across every placement — from React developers to travel nurses. That's not a pitch. That's just how it evolved, because it's what makes sense.
